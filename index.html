What I Wish I Knew as an Undergrad

  • The Value of Liberal Arts — Rhodes gives you a unique opportunity to take any class, regardless of major. Start early.
  • Taking Hard Classes > Perfect GPA — Some of my most valuable learning experiences were in classes I didn't get an A in.
  • Side Projects and Self Learing — My marathon research got me into school and got me my current role. It's also one of the experiences where I learned the most in undergrad.
  • Internships Help Eliminate Options — Any experience is always valuable. There is always something transferable, and you'll learn more about what you prioritize in an opportunity.

SQL: Your Daily Driver

Why SQL Matters

  • Foundation for everything you build
  • Interface between applications
  • Get data in, store data out
  • Answer most questions with GROUP, SUM, AVG

Example

-- Player distance across sessions
SELECT 
    player_id,
    SUM(total_distance) as total,
    COUNT(DISTINCT session_id)
FROM player_drills
WHERE player_id = 'PLAYER_123'
GROUP BY player_id;

Statistics You Actually Use

Catching Bad Data: Heart Rate

  • GPS device loses contact when improperly fitted and skew session averages
  • Compare average HR to expected range for that training load
  • Flag sessions where avg HR is unusually high for similar effort
  • Understanding "normal" vs "outlier" catches the problem

Stats aren't theoretical. Understanding distributions and standard deviation is how you protect data quality daily.

Data Visualization Principles

Pass Network Visualization

  • Complex, match-specific analysis
  • Looks impressive in presentations
  • Takes significant time to build
  • Reality: Rarely gets checked by tactical staff

Data Visualization Principles

Player Drill Summary Table

  • Simple, sortable table with key metrics
  • All information in one view
  • Quick to build and update
  • Reality: Used by performance staff daily

Simple beats fancy. Build for frequency of use, not to impress.
